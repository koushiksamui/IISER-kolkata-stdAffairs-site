Our purpose

Mission & vision

Mission

To provide every IISER student with safe housing, accessible healthcare, sound mental health support, and the administrative backing needed to pursue academics and personal growth without hindrance — delivered with fairness, transparency, and care.

Vision

A campus where student wellbeing is treated as seriously as academic excellence — where every student, regardless of background, has equal access to opportunity, support, and a genuine sense of belonging.

Dean of Students' Affairs
Prof. A. Krishnan
A message from the Dean

College is more than coursework — it's where you learn to live independently, build community, and ask for help when you need it.

When I took on this role, my one commitment was that no student at IISER should ever feel like they are navigating a crisis alone — whether that's a hostel dispute, a health concern, a financial constraint, or something harder to name.

Over the years, this office has grown from a small administrative desk into a full support network: wardens, counsellors, medical staff, committee members, and student volunteers, all working toward the same goal. We still have work to do, and we welcome your feedback at every step.

My door — and this office's helpline — stays open around the clock. Please use it.

Our journey

Milestones

'08

Office established

The Students' Affairs Office was formed to centralize hostel administration and student welfare under one roof.

'14

Counselling Cell launched

Dedicated mental health professionals joined the campus for the first time, offering free and confidential sessions.

'18

ICC & Anti-Ragging committees strengthened

Formal, student-facing reporting channels were introduced along with mandatory orientation for all incoming batches.

'22

24/7 helpline goes live

A round-the-clock support line was introduced for emergencies, safety concerns, and mental health crises.

'26

Digital-first services

Hostel allotment, scholarship applications, and grievance filing moved online for faster, more transparent processing.
