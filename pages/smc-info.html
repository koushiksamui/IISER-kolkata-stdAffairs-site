Who we are

What the SMC does

Elected student representatives from every hostel work alongside the mess contractor and SAO to keep dining fair, hygienic, and responsive to student input.

Menu & Quality Oversight

Reviews and approves the weekly menu, monitors ingredient quality, and runs monthly tasting panels with student volunteers.

Hygiene Audits

Conducts surprise kitchen inspections and publishes hygiene scores for every mess hall on this page.

Feedback & Grievances

Collects and escalates student feedback, food safety complaints, and billing disputes to the mess contractor and SAO.

Tell us how we're doing

Mess feedback & complaints

Rate a meal, flag a hygiene concern, or raise a billing dispute — the SMC reviews every submission at its weekly meeting.

How feedback is handled

  • Hygiene and food-safety complaints are escalated to the contractor within 24 hours.
  • Menu suggestions are discussed at the SMC's weekly meeting with hostel representatives.
  • Billing disputes are routed to the Hostel Administrative Office for resolution.
  • Aggregated ratings are published monthly on this page for transparency.

Common questions

SMC FAQs

Mess fees are billed as a fixed advance per semester alongside your hostel fee, based on a per-meal rate approved jointly by the SMC and the contractor at the start of each academic year.

Students on approved medical leave or official institute travel for 5+ consecutive days can apply for a pro-rated mess rebate through their hostel warden.

Representatives are elected by residents of each hostel at the start of every academic year. Nominations open alongside hostel council elections — watch the notice board for dates.

All mess halls offer a daily vegetarian and non-vegetarian option, and a dedicated Jain/no-onion-garlic counter. For medical dietary needs, contact your mess hall's SMC representative directly.

Have a mess-related concern?

Reach your hostel's SMC representative directly, or explore hostel management resources.
